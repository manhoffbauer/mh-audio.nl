Calculate Print Size with givem DPI


The size of a photo is displayed in pixels. For example, your camera takes a photo of 2400 x 1600. Then the photo is 2400 pixels wide and 1600 pixels high. These dimensions partly determine the size of your photo in kilobytes or megabytes (kB or MB).

Resolution is the amount of information in your photo per cm, i.e. the number of pixels per cm. By default, the unit 'inch' is used instead of cm.

Combine the previous two paragraphs and the conclusion is simple.

At a resolution of 72 dpi (actually ppi), the 2400 x 1600 pixel photo is 33 inches wide and 22 inches high. For example, the width consists of 2400 pixels and if 72 pixels are displayed on one inch, then 33 inches are needed for 2400 pixels. The same of course applies to the height.

At a resolution of 300 dpi (actually ppi), the 2400 x 1600 pixel photo is 8 inches wide and 5.3 inches high.

To print a photo properly, 300 dpi is usually the right resolution.

On the other hand, to properly display a photo on the screen, a resolution of 72 dpi is often sufficient.
