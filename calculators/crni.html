Acrylic Bending Machine

( Heating bending )




Bending Plexiglass offers a range of possibilities because this material softens when heated without discolouring. This allows you to make the most beautiful things from it! From a transparent bedside table or a colored plexiglass cabinet to a handy laptop holder or tablet holder.

As it becomes warm it softens and can be bent or formed into any shape imaginable. The typical forming temperature range of most acrylic sheet plastics is 275°F to 350°F (135°C to 177°C). A consistent and controlled heat source is necessary so that the bends are smooth and the finished parts are aesthetically pleasing.

Turn the sheet of acrylic over on the heating element every 30-60 seconds in order to avoid damaging it. Wear gloves as you do this to avoid burning your hands.
Only keep the acrylic on the strip heater long enough for it to become flexible, at which point it is ready for you to bend.
It may be helpful to clamp the acrylic in place while it is being heated so that only the marked area is heated and the sheet of acrylic does not move.

Preparations for drilling in Plexiglas

When drilling in Plexiglas, it is important that the plate is well supported. Place the plate on a flat surface that supports the plate well and in which the drill can run out, for example a wooden plate. If possible, you can also clamp the plate along the borehole / bore holes, you do this by laying one or more slats along the drill holes and clamping with glue trays. Do you want to drill holes along the edge of the plate? Then the distance between the edge of the plate and the edge of the borehole must be at least twice the plate thickness. If the plate is 4 millimeters thick, then the distance between the edge and the edge of the hole is 8 millimeters.

Do not draw the drill holes directly on the plexiglass or on the protective film. Always stick painting tape on the plate and draw the drill holes on it. It is advisable to also stick the plate on the underlying side with painting tape, especially with thinner plates (thinner than 5 millimeters).

You can drill in Plexiglas with a normal iron drill or an HSS drill. It is important that the drill is not brand new, the sharp cutting corner can cause the drill to "bite" in the plate, causing the plate to tear out. If you drill a few holes in wood or aluminum, the sharp side is already gone and you can use the drill for drilling in Plexiglas.

Place the drill perpendicular to the Plexiglas plate and immediately let the drill run at medium -high speed. The chip must break down immediately, if this is not the case, the drill turns too slowly. Just press light on the drill, the drill must lower the material itself. Support the drill well if it is almost through the plate, and let the drill run into the underlying plate. Let the drill turn while you pull the drill out of the hole.
